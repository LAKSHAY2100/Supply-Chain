AI-Powered Resilient Supply Chain
Intelligence System

with Perishable-Aware Decision Optimization (Avocado Use Case)

1. Problem Statement

Modern global supply chains manage millions of concurrent shipments across highly complex
and volatile transportation networks.
Disruptions such as:

     Weather events
     Traffic congestion
     Port delays
     Infrastructure bottlenecks
are typically detected after delivery timelines are already compromised.

Core Challenge

        Supply chains lack systems that can continuously analyze real-time data,
        predict disruptions early, and dynamically adapt decisions before failures
        occur.

Critical Gaps in Existing Systems

    1. Reactive Decision-Making
          Systems respond after disruptions occur

    2. No Continuous Intelligence
          Lack of real-time adaptive analysis

    3. No Impact Awareness
          Inability to predict cascading (domino) effects
    4. No Value-Based Optimization
          Focus on delivery time, not outcome quality

Additional Gap (Perishable Goods)

For perishable commodities like avocados:
        A delay does not just affect time -- it directly impacts product quality and economic
        value.

2. Objective

To design a scalable, AI-powered system that:
     Continuously analyzes multi-source transit data
     Predicts disruptions before they occur
     Dynamically optimizes routes in real-time
     Simulates cascading supply chain impacts
     Integrates quality-aware intelligence for perishables
     Provides explainable decision outputs

3. Proposed Solution

Resilient Supply Chain Intelligence System

A real-time, self-adaptive decision intelligence platform that operates on:
        TRACK  ANALYZE  PREDICT  SIMULATE  DECIDE 
        EXECUTE/RECOMMEND  EXPLAIN

Key Innovation
        The system optimizes not just delivery efficiency, but the final value of goods at
        arrival, incorporating perishable-aware intelligence.

4. System Architecture

 1. Data Layer

 2. Continuous Intelligence Layer (AI/ML Core)

A. Disruption Prediction Engine

     Models:
               Random Forest (MVP)
               LSTM (advanced)

     Outputs:
               Delay probability
               Risk classification

B. Quality Prediction Engine (Perishable-Aware Layer)

(Using avocado as demonstration)
Predicts:
     Remaining shelf life
     Quality score (0ｭ100%)
     Spoilage probability
Based on:
     Time
     Temperature
     Delay

         
         The Core Formula This is the mathematical heart of the demo. Quality decays based on
         time and temperature. The formula is simple, defensible, and directly maps to real
         perishable goods research: Quality(t) = Q_initial ﾗ exp( -k ﾗ T_excess ﾗ t ) Where: k =
         decay constant (0.03 for avocados) T_excess = max(0, truck_temp - ideal_temp) (ideal =
         6ｰC) t = hours in transit 
         

                                                                                                                                           
C. Real-Time Data Processing

     Continuously updates:
               Risk scores

 3. Graph-Based Optimization Layer

Supply chain modeled as:
     Nodes  ports, warehouses, hubs
     Edges  transport routes

Edge Weights Include:

     Travel time
     Cost
     Risk score
     Quality degradation factor

Algorithms:

     Dijkstra (baseline)
     A* (optimized routing)

         
         THE COMPOSITE WEIGHT FORMULA The composite edge weight formula is: weight =
         (w1 ﾗ time) + (w2 ﾗ cost/1000) + (w3 ﾗ decay_rate ﾗ 100). Default weights: w1=0.4
         (time), w2=0.3 (cost), w3=0.3 (quality). When a disruption is detected, the disrupted
         node's edge weight is set to 9999 -- making Dijkstra automatically avoid it without any
         conditional logic.
